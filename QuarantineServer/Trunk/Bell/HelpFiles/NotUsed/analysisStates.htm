Analysis States

The AntiVirus Immune System network reports the status of samples that have been submitted as they progress through the analysis process with the X-Analysis-State attribute. The state may indicate where in the network hierarchy a sample is located, or what stage of the analysis pipeline is currently working on the sample, or its final disposition.

Final States
Samples that have been finished are in one of the final states. All nodes in the Immune System use the terminal states. After a sample has been placed in a terminal state, its state will not change again. The X-Date-Analyzed attribute is set when a sample is placed into a terminalstate; its presence means that the value of X-Analysis-State is terminal.

  • uninfectable: The sample contains no executable code, and therefore cannot be infected with any virus. The sample may be too small to contain any executable code, or may contain data only, such as a graphic image or an audio clip.
  • unsubmitable: The sample contains known malicious software which is not a virus, such as a trojan horse.
  • declined: The sample is not acceptable, and has been politely but firmly refused.
  • nodetect: The sample has not been analyzed, but does not contain any apparent suspicious code.
  • infected: The sample is infected with a virus, and can be repaired with available virus definition files.
  • uninfected: The sample has been analyzed and no virus was found.
  • misfired: The sample has been analyzed and no virus was found, in spite of a detected infection. The incorrectly detected infection was caused by a mistake in previous virus definition files, and the mistake is corrected in newer virus definition files.
  • abort: An internal programming error has derailed transport or analysis of the sample.
  • broken: The sample is infected with a virus, but the definition generation service in the back office reported an error, and no virus definition files are available.

Transit States
Samples that have not yet reached the analysis center are in one of the transit states. Only components outside the analysis center use the transit states. A sample may remain in a pending state indefinitely before moving to another state.

  • receiving: The sample is being received by a gateway.
  • accepted: The sample has been accepted by a gateway, but not yet imported into the analysis center.
  • importing: The sample is being imported into the analysis center.

Pending States
Samples that are awaiting analysis within the analysis center are in one of the pending states. Only components within the analysis center use the pending states. A sample may remain in a pending state indefinitely before moving to another state.

  • defer: The sample cannot be analyzed automatically, and will be deferred for analysis by experts.
  • deferred: The sample cannot be analyzed automatically, and has been deferred for analysis by experts.
  • deferring: The sample cannot be analyzed automatically, and is being deferred for analysis by experts.
  • imported: The sample has been imported into the analysis center, but has not yet been analyzed.
  • rescan: The sample must be re-scanned because newer virus definition files have become available within the analysis center.

Active States
Samples that are being analyzed within the analysis center are in one of the active states. Only the dataflow component within the analysis center uses the active states. A sample may remain in an active state for only a few seconds or for many minutes before moving to another state.

  • classifying: The sample is being classified to determine its datatype.
  • binary: The sample has been classified as a binary program, and is waiting for the binary controller.
  • binaryControlling: The binary controller is determining starting conditions for binary replication.
  • binaryWait: The sample is waiting for a binary replication engine to become available.
  • binaryReplicating: The sample is being executed by a binary replication engine.
  • binaryScoring: The sample infected other binary programs, and the binary scoring engine is selecting signatures for detecting and repairing the virus.
  • macro: The sample has been classified as a document or spreadsheet containing executable macros, and is waiting for the macro controller.
  • macroControlling: The macro controller is determining starting conditions for macro replication.
  • macroWait: The sample is waiting for a macro replication engine to become available.
  • macroReplicating: The sample is being executed by a macro replication engine.
  • macroScoring: The sample infected other documents or spreadsheets, and the macro scoring engine is selecting signatures for detecting and repairing the virus.
  • signatures: The sample is infected with a new virus, signatures for detecting and repairing it have been selected, and the sample is waiting for the build process to become available.
  • locking: Exclusive access to the definition generation service in the back office is being acquired.
  • incrBuilding: The signatures selected for the new virus are being added to the current virus definition files.
  • incrUnitTesting: The incremental virus definition files are being unit-tested.
  • fullBuilding: A new set of virus definition files incorporating the signatures selected for the new virus are being built.
  • fullUnitTesting: The full virus definition files are being unit-tested.
  • unlocking: Exclusive access to the definition generation service is being released.
  • archive: The sample is waiting to archive the automated analysis files.
  • archiving: The sample is archiving the automated analysis files.
